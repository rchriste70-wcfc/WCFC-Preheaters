# Preheater Program Charter  
**Wings of Carolina Flying Club**

**Version:** 1.1  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Review Cycle:** Annual (prior to cold-weather season)

---

## 1. Purpose

The Preheater Program ensures safe, reliable, and consistent preheating
of Wings of Carolina Flying Club aircraft engines during cold-weather
operations.

Proper preheating reduces engine wear, improves safety of flight, and
protects club aircraft assets.

---

## 2. Scope

This charter applies to:
- All club-owned preheaters (currently two units)
- All club members operating aircraft when ambient air temperature is below 40°F
- All activities related to preheater storage, fueling, inspection,
  maintenance, and communication

This charter does not supersede FAA regulations, aircraft POHs, insurance
requirements, or airport rules.

---

## 3. Policy Statement

- Preheating is **required** prior to engine start when ambient air
  temperature is below **40°F**, unless otherwise authorized by the Board
  or Head of Maintenance.
- Aircraft shall not be started cold if preheaters are unavailable,
  inoperative, or unsafe to use.
- Fuel misuse (e.g., gasoline or 100LL) is strictly prohibited.

---

## 4. Program Ownership & Authority

### 4.1 Preheater Program Volunteer
The Preheater Program Volunteer is responsible for day-to-day operation
of the program on behalf of the club.

### 4.2 Authority
The Preheater Program Volunteer may:
- Declare a preheater **out of service**
- Temporarily restrict use for safety or maintenance
- Recommend policy or equipment changes, subject to approval by the
  **Board of Directors and Club President**

The **Head of Maintenance** provides technical oversight and coordination.

Ultimate authority resides with the Board of Directors.

---

## 5. Assets Covered

- Two (2) portable aircraft preheaters
- Accessories (cords, hoses, inserts, covers)
- Fuel containers and approved fuel

Each unit shall be uniquely identifiable and tracked.

---

## 6. Responsibilities

### Preheater Program Volunteer
- Maintain operational readiness
- Manage inventory and consumables
- Maintain the program calendar
- Communicate with members
- Escalate issues appropriately
- Keep documentation current

### Club Members
- Follow published procedures
- Report issues promptly
- Respect out-of-service designations

### Board of Directors
- Approve policy and material changes
- Support funding and replacements
- Resolve escalations

---

## 7. Operating Standards

- Approved fuel only
- Maintain fuel level at or above ¼ tank
- Perform inspections and functional tests
- Remove equipment from service if unsafe

---

## 8. Seasonal Operations

Season kickoff, in-season inspections, and shutdown activities are
defined in the Preheater Program Calendar.

---

## 9. Communication Artifacts

- Program overview
- Quick Start instructions
- Seasonal communications
- Issue-reporting mechanism
- Out-of-service tags

---

## 10. Safety & Risk Management

Key risks include:
- Engine damage from cold starts
- Fuel misuse
- Fire or carbon monoxide hazards
- Equipment unavailability

Mitigation relies on standardization, inspection, communication, and
authority clarity.

---

## 11. Documentation & Recordkeeping

All program documentation is stored in a centralized location accessible
to current and future volunteers and the Board.

---

## 12. Succession & Transition

The program is role-based. Upon transition:
- Documentation and inventory are reviewed
- A handoff briefing is conducted
- Board acknowledgment is recorded

---

## 13. Review & Amendments

- Reviewed annually
- Amendments require Board approval
- Minor operational updates may be made with Board notification
